About Me

2019 Graduate of Knox College with a B.A. major in Physics and minor in Computer Science and Astronomy.

Physics, in its most fundamental form, embodies the science of probability and prediction within the physical realm. Hypotheses emerge from the foundational blocks of preceding knowledge, while discoveries serve as springboards for further exploration. Our ideas continuously evolve, paralleled by the refinement of the technologies that facilitate their conception.

To me, the paramount endeavor of our lives is fostering growth and development. This ethos underlines our potential to enhance both our individual lives and the world at large. The rapid stride of technological progress since the turn of the millennium has been nothing short of extraordinary. However, this is to the point where imparting these innovations will inevitably grow more challenging.

I've witnessed this swift evolution of technology in the classroom right in front of me. Complex AI models that can write entire essays, translate language, and even solve complex math problems, are all recent developments that drastically change education. This is why the increasing significance of AI is imperative to the future of education and holds the transformative power to revolutionize our learning processes. Not just true in reference to the classroom, but to multiple industries such as medical, industrial, and business.
